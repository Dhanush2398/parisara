Pollution board makes STP compliance a pricey affair

  • 31 Mar 2021
  • (03)

Charges for obtaining consent for establishment and operation for sewage treatment plants at apartment complexes hiked to between Rs 2,000-10 lakh

The Karnataka State Pollution Control Board (KSPCB) recently issued a circular with revised charges for obtaining consent for establishment (CFE) and consent for operation (CFO) for apartment complexes that ought to have sewage treatment plants (STPs). The annual fees vary between Rs 2,000 and Rs 10 lakh, covering both standalone and integrated projects. Now, residents are wondering why the regulatory body is charging apartment owners for compliance.

The circular, which follows a government notification issued in November last year, is applicable for all STPs including the BWSSB-owned plants and private ones operated by apartments, hospitals, IT parks and others. While the CFE is a one-time charge, the KSPCB will levy fees for obtaining CFO annually, followed by an inspection of the plant.

As per the revised charges, apartment complexes that comprise fewer than 14 houses are expected to pay Rs 2,000 for obtaining the consent for operation, annually. The charges for apartments with 15 to 71 houses and 72 to 140 houses are Rs 10,000 and Rs 20,000 respectively. Bigger apartments have to shell out more. Apartment complexes with 141 to 429 houses have to pay Rs 50,000.

The annual charges for integrated projects are almost double as compared to standalone apartments. Integrated projects are the ones that comprise two or more components such as residential, commercial, hotel, education institutions, hospital, club, mall and others. "If the CFO is rejected by KSPCB, the proponent has to pay the CFO once again when he or she re-applies," the order states.

The charges are extremely high. Apartments are already spending a reasonably high cost on maintaining STPs

Nagesh Aras, a resident of Bellandur, termed the steep hike in charges as highly unfair and against the polluter pays principle enshrined in National Environment Policy. "The apartment residents are already paying through their nose for the operations and maintenance of the STP. Instead of incentivising, the KSPCB is imposing additional charges on the apartments. This is unfortunate," he said. It's learnt that some of the apartments have already paid the revised charges as their STPs were due for annual inspection.

Vikram Rai, treasurer at Bangalore Apartments Federation urged the KSPCB to reduce the charges and make compliance of the rules more attractive.

"The charges are extremely high. Apartments are already spending a reasonably high cost on maintaining the STPs. We are not able to understand the logic behind KSPCB charging apartments for compliance without providing a service," he said. The federation, he said, already had one round of meeting with officials. "We will continue to pursue avenues to raise objections to the steep hike," he said.

Srinivasalu, member secretary at KSPCB justified the move stating that the charges were revised for the first time since the last 20 years. "We are an autonomous body. In order to sustain our operational cost and continue our regulatory services, we need some avenues to raise revenue," he said. "The charges are levied in exchange of inspecting the plant annually. The charges are not steep if it is calculated per each house basis instead of each apartment complex," he said.

Meanwhile, Aras stated that the KSPCB could meet its operational expenditure by imposing penalties on apartments or commercial buildings that do not comply with the rules and pollute the water bodies by discharging sewage. He wondered why the apartment which is complying with all rules is being made to pay high charges for getting consent for operation every year.
