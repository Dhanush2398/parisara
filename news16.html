ACB’s record since its inception in 2016

The Anti-Corruption Bureau (ACB) has registered 1,356 first information reports (FIRs) across the state since its inception in 2016 but it has succeeded only in four cases to get the guilty punished. While social activists say this is not a good record, ACB sleuths say the process is long and winding. The state government has spent Rs 119 crore towards salaries and other expenses for the ACB sleuths in five years and half of the amount (Rs 60.50 crore) has been spent on ACB staff in Bengaluru.

According to a RTI document, ACB sleuths have registered 186 disproportionate assets (DA) cases and 957 bribery cases between 2016 and 2020 in other districts in the state while 213 FIRs were registered in Bengaluru. However, the RTI document has not specified how many DA cases and bribery cases are among the 213 FIRs registered in Bengaluru in the last five years. In another RTI reply, the ACB said that it is yet to file charge sheets in 14 cases registered since 2017 in Bengaluru Rural District. The ACB has registered 32 cases from 2017 to 2020 in Bengaluru Rural District and charge sheets were filed in 18 of such cases. "The investigation is in progress in 14 trap cases and charge sheets were filed in 18 trap cases in Bengaluru Rural district. No 'B' report was filed in any of these trap cases," said ACB Police Inspector T R Kumaraswamy in a RTI reply to Sai Datta, a social activist.

Meanwhile, Seemant Kumar Singh, Additional Director General of Police (ADGP) of Anti-Corruption Bureau said that the ACB police need sufficient time to charge-sheet corrupt government officials caught in the DA cases. "Four to five years is too little time to charge-sheet government officials caught in DA cases. There are a lot of processes and formalities to follow and people to be contacted, ncluding chartered accountants and advocates representing the accused. Our staff, including advocates representing the ACB, have Deen working hard to ensure punishment for the guilty," said Seemant Kumar Singh. According to him, the ACB sleuths have filed charge-sheets in 10 to 12 cases. However, social activists want the ACB sleuths to work harder and expedite the process of filing charge-sheets against more corrupt government officials and employees. "It seems the ACB authorities have all resources. It is the responsibility of the ACB police to prepare charge-sheets without any delay," said Sai Datta, a social activist. Differing with the ADGP, Ravi Krishna Reddy of Karnataka Rashtra Samiti wonders what has prevented the ACB police from preparing Charge-sheets against those caught while accepting bribes. "There is no need for the ACB police to seek more time to file charge-sheets against those government employees caught while accepting bribes from the public," said Ravi Krishna Reddy.
